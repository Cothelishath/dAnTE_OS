Activity Overlaps
Where two activities share a block without either being diminished — and where they cannot

Not all overlaps are equal. Some are intentional and productive — active listening while doing physical work, for example. Others are traps that disguise distraction as efficiency. The following have been specifically designed into the timetable and genuinely enhance rather than compete with each other.

🏋
Workout + Audio (Sermon / Podcast / Jim Rohn)
Physical movement requires no cognitive bandwidth for the activity itself — your mind is free to receive. This is one of the most efficient double-use blocks in the day. Body moves, mind feeds. Neither is diminished.
→ 30 min workout = 10 AP body + 15 AP sermon toward daily target. 25 AP in one block.
📊
Deep Chart Work + Sermon Audio
Chart study is visual and analytical. Sermon is auditory and spiritual. They use different cognitive channels entirely. The 10:30 and 15:00 blocks are designed for this pairing. Over a week this covers your 3-hour sermon target without requiring a dedicated block.
→ 90 min chart session = 20 AP charts + 15 AP sermon. Each session advances both targets simultaneously.
🌅
Morning Prayer + Worship Playlist
The playlist is not entertainment — it is atmosphere. It sets the tone, reduces friction of entering prayer, and provides a gentle soundtrack for communion. This is not multitasking: the music serves the prayer, it does not compete with it.
→ One press the night before. Morning-you walks into something already alive and prepared.
🍳
Meal Prep / Eating + Light Podcast
Meals require minimal attention. A podcast running during breakfast or dinner prep uses otherwise empty cognitive space. Keep it light — Vusi or a finance podcast. Not trading analysis, which requires full visual focus.
→ 20 min lunch prep = 12–15 AP podcast. No cost to either activity.
🌙
Evening Wind-down + Remaining Sermon
As you wind down — light tidying, evening routine, lying still — sermon audio continues in background. This completes your daily sermon target without requiring a dedicated focused evening block. Cumulative across morning, afternoon and evening.
→ Sermon target met in pieces across the day. No single block required to carry the full load.
🔄
Transition Time + Charts Visible
During the morning transition — dishes, bath, dressing — the desktop is already on and charts are loading. You are not yet working, but the environment is preparing itself. When you sit down at 7am, there is zero friction to beginning. The market is already alive on your screen.
→ Preparation happens during unavoidable transition. Arrival at desk = immediate engagement.
⚠️
Overlaps to Avoid
Chart work and book reading compete for the same analytical attention — both suffer. Prayer and phone browsing eliminates prayer entirely. Deep career work and a podcast that requires active listening dilutes both. Bible reading and anything else reduces retention and cheapens the text. These are not overlaps — they are compromises dressed as efficiency.
→ Rule: if both activities require active cognitive engagement, they cannot share a block. One must be passive or physical. Choose one to receive your full attention.
