This is not a planning document. It is a clarity document. The questions here are not asking you to perform certainty — they are asking you to sit with the things you have not yet put into words. Answer honestly, not well. Vague answers are permitted only as placeholders; they should be revisited and sharpened. The document is private, living, and never finished. It grows as you do.

Section I
Intent
Before anything else, the question underneath every other question: do you actually want this? Not the idea of it, not the comfort of it, not the resolution of missing her. The real thing, with its weight and its demands and its particular shape. Answer this section before the rest. Come back to it after the rest.
Why do you want to get back with Tamara? Write every reason, including the ones that don't sound noble.
Honest
Is any part of this decision driven by financial inconvenience — the cost of pursuing a relationship, the cost of visiting, of gifting, of eventually building a shared life? Sit with this honestly. What is the honest answer?
Honest
What would your life look like in five years if you didn't pursue this? Not as a threat or a manipulation — as a genuine alternative. What does the version of you without Tamara look like?
Hard
Is the aversion to re-entering a relationship a genuine need for freedom, or is it the comfort of not being accountable to another person's needs? Be precise about which it is — or whether both are true simultaneously.
Honest
What does a relationship with Tamara require of you that your current single life does not? List the specific demands — emotional, financial, temporal, spiritual. Then ask yourself whether you are willing to meet them.
Measurable
Do you love her? Not in the abstract — specifically. What do you love about Tamara Dogubo that you could not say about another woman?
Honest
If the financial pressure were entirely removed — the account was full, the debt was gone, the apartment was complete — would your desire to be with her change? More urgent? Less? The same?
Hard
What are you afraid of — specifically — about being in this relationship again?

Section II
The Audit
What happened the first time. Not the story you've told people, not the version that distributes blame evenly for politeness — the honest account of what you did, what you didn't do, and what it cost her. And what she brought that was difficult. This section is not about guilt. It is about pattern recognition.
What were her specific complaints the first time? List them all, as accurately as you can remember them — not summarised, not softened.
Honest
For each complaint she had — were they valid? What specifically made them valid or not? Do not defend yourself here. Examine.
Honest
What did the financial pressure specifically do to your behaviour in the relationship? Be concrete — what did it change, what did it suppress, what did it produce in you that she experienced?
Measurable
Where did you fail to initiate? Conflict resolution, planning, spiritual leadership, reaching out. Not once — as a pattern. What did the pattern look like and what drove it?
Honest
What did you withhold from her — information, emotion, plans, fears — and why? What did the withholding cost the relationship?
Honest
What did she bring that was genuinely difficult for you? Not as a counter-argument — as an honest account of what you found hard. What challenged you about her?
Honest
What patterns in the relationship repeated more than once? What did you see coming back around that you never addressed at the root?
Measurable
What did the two months of silence after the break reveal to you — about yourself, about her, about what the relationship was and what it wasn't?
Hard
If Tamara were answering these questions about you — what would she say were your three most significant failures in the relationship? Do you agree with her assessment?
Hard
Section III
Your Growth
What has actually changed. Not what you intend to change, not what you plan to work on — what has concretely shifted in you in the two months since. The distinction matters: she will not be returning to a man of good intentions. She will be returning to a man of demonstrated change. What evidence exists?
What has materially changed in your life since the relationship ended? The job, the apartment, the debt plan. What do these changes actually mean for who you are in a relationship now versus then?
Measurable
For each specific complaint she had — what have you concretely done differently in the two months since? Not what you plan to do. What you have actually done, even without her present to see it.
Measurable
What have you learned about yourself in the two months since — through the move, the job, the solitude, the faith practice — that you did not know about yourself before?
Honest
The pattern you identified in yourself — excessive flexibility with self, letting the hook go — how does this pattern manifest in a relationship specifically? And what are you doing about it?
Honest
What have you not worked on yet that you know you need to? Name it plainly. Not as a confession — as a plan.
Honest
How are you different in conflict now than you were twelve months ago? Give a specific example — real or hypothetical — of how you would handle something differently.
Measurable
What are you currently doing — in your daily life, your routine, your faith practice — that is preparing you to be the man she needs? Not planning to do. Doing.
Measurable
Section IV
Her
What you want from Tamara, what you are prepared to offer her, and what you genuinely need her to bring. Not the romantic version — the honest architecture of what this relationship requires from both sides to actually work.
What do you specifically need from Tamara in this relationship — not preferences, not nice-to-haves, but the things whose sustained absence would hollow the relationship out?
Honest
What are you prepared to do for her — concretely, measurably — that you were not doing before? Not character improvements. Actions. What will she see that she didn't see?
Measurable
Her BPD is a real dimension of who she is. What does loving someone with BPD require of you specifically — not in general terms, but in daily practice — and are you genuinely prepared to provide it?
Honest
What do you expect her to have worked on or changed in the two months of silence? What would you need to see from her before you feel ready to trust the dynamic again?
Honest
What are you not willing to accept this time that you tolerated before? Draw the line clearly. Not as an ultimatum to deliver — as a clarity for yourself.
Honest
How do you plan to handle the phone time tension honestly — her need for connection and your need for capacity — without one of you silently resenting the other?
Measurable
What does Tamara need that you have historically been bad at providing — and what is your specific, actionable plan for each one?
Measurable
What conversation do you need to have with Tamara before or early in re-entering — things that need to be said clearly, debts acknowledged, expectations named? What are you going to say and how?
Honest

How will you handle conflict in this relationship? Specifically — what is the protocol when something is wrong, when you notice a shift, when she comes to you dysregulated? Not values. Steps.
Measurable
How will finances work in this relationship — who pays for what, at what stage, under what expectations? Be specific about the current stage and the intended progression.
Measurable
How will you protect your morning routine — the 4AM, the prayer block, the trading sessions, the building — while also being present and available to her? What is the actual structure?
Measurable
How will you build a shared spiritual life — not alongside each other but together? What does that actually look like in a long-distance relationship and then in the same city?

The architecture of the long horizon. Not wishes — decisions in formation. Where you are going, when, and what shapes the path. Timelines are not prisons — they are directions. They tell you whether you are moving or standing still.

Measurable
Where will you live — and what dictates that decision? Her career, your career, family, dAnTE Inc., quality of life, cost of living. How does Port Harcourt vs Lagos vs elsewhere get resolved?
Measurable
In one year from now, what does the relationship look like concretely — same city, still long distance, engaged, frequency of visits, financial state, shared plans?
Measurable
In three years — where are you, where is she, what are you building together, what has the relationship become?
Measurable
In five years — what does the household look like? Children? Where? What is each of you building professionally and for dAnTE Inc.? What role does she play in or alongside the vision?
Measurable
In ten years — what have you built together, what kind of household have you created, what does she know about herself and the world because she has been with you, and what do you know because you have been with her?
Measurable
In twenty years — what does the legacy of this marriage look like? What have the two of you built together that would not have existed otherwise? What do your children or the people around you carry because of what you built?
Measurable
What role does Tamara play in dAnTE Inc. — as partner, as supporter, as independent builder alongside it? How does her creative career intersect with or run alongside the conglomerate vision?
Measurable
What needs to happen financially before you can support a shared household — and what is the timeline for getting there? Be specific about the numbers and the plan.
Measurable
Children — do you want them, when, how many, and what kind of father do you intend to be? What needs to be true in the marriage before that is the right moment?
Honest
What would cause this relationship to not work even if you both gave it everything? What are the conditions under which it should end — clearly defined, not vague?

Section VII
Faith & Foundation
Faith is not a department of your life — it is the ground beneath everything. This section asks what that means specifically in the context of this relationship. Not principles you affirm. Practices you embody.
Have you prayed specifically about Tamara — not for her, but about whether this relationship is right? What has come from that? What has God said, shown, or been silent about?
Faith
What does it mean to be the spiritual head of this relationship — practically, daily? What does it look like when you are doing it well and when you are failing at it?
Faith
Is Tamara a woman whose faith is compatible with the life you are building — not identical, but moving in the same direction? What do you know about her relationship with God and what does it tell you?
Faith
How will you build a shared prayer life — from where you are now, across distance, and then in the same household? What does that progression look like?
Faith
What does it mean to love Tamara the way God intends — not the romantic ideal, but the covenant commitment? What does that cost and are you willing to pay it?
Faith
What role does faith play in how you would handle the hard moments — her BPD episodes, financial pressure, distance, disagreement? How does your belief actively shape your response rather than just your values?
Faith
What do you believe God thinks of this relationship — this specific one, with this specific woman, at this specific point in your life? Not what you hope He thinks. What discernment have you received or are you seeking?

Section VIII
You First
Who you are independent of this relationship. What you are building for yourself, what your life looks like on its own terms, where you are going with or without anyone else in the picture. A man who needs a relationship to function is a different man from one who chooses a relationship from fullness. This section is about which man you are right now.
Where are you right now — honestly, completely? Financial position, emotional state, faith practice, trading discipline, professional clarity, physical health. The full picture, as of today.
Honest
What are your non-negotiable personal goals for the next twelve months — independent of any relationship? What must happen for you to consider the year a success on your own terms?
Measurable
Where do you want to be in three years — professionally, financially, physically, spiritually — entirely on your own terms? What is the version of Paul at 30-something that you are building toward?
Measurable
What does dAnTE Inc. look like in five years — specifically? Which entities are operational, what revenue, what team, what impact? This is the life's work. Name it concretely.
Measurable
What kind of man do you want to be at fifty? Not what you want to have — who you want to be. What does the character, the faith, the presence of Paul Bassey at fifty look like?
Honest
What is the dAnTE Foundation — specifically? What does the educational institution look like, who does it serve, where does it operate, what does it produce in the young people who pass through it? This is the legacy. Name it.
Measurable
What does financial independence look like for you — specifically, in numbers? What is the trading account size, the Dante Craft revenue, the passive income that tells you the constraint is gone?
Measurable
What are the things you are afraid of — for yourself, about your own life, independent of any relationship? Name the fears that live in the quiet.
Hard
What do you need to become before you would feel you have no excuse left — before you could stand before God and say: I gave this life everything it deserved?
