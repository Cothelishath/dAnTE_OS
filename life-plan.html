The Ground
Foundation
Everything in this document stands on what is written here. The faith, the values, the operating principles. These do not change because circumstances change. They are the thing that makes the changes in circumstance navigable. Write them, own them, return to them.
The Faith Statement
Faith is not a department of my life. It is the ground beneath everything else — not a value among values but the condition that makes all other values possible, coherent, and directional. I am in active relationship with a living God, and every plan in this document is held in that relationship, subject to His direction, and dependent on His strength.
The Four Anchors
Anchor I
Individual Responsibility to Self
Circumstances are context, not verdict. The obligation to become, to build, and to show up fully belongs entirely to the individual. Circumstances are information. They are not permission to stop.
Anchor II
A Full Present Life
The long horizon and the present moment are not in competition. The small days are not the price of the life — they are the life. The life is now, compounding.
Anchor III
Autonomy
Freedom from constraint in all its forms. The goal is not accumulation but the capacity to choose — what to do, what to refuse, how to give. Build toward freedom, then use the freedom to build toward others.
Anchor IV
Self-Possession and Coherence
To be the same person in every room. Thought, word, action, and presentation consistent with each other. What cannot be backed up should not be claimed. What is claimed must be delivered.
Operating Principles
Begin with God. The quality of every downstream hour is shaped by the first hour.
Circumstances are the starting point, not the ceiling.
Do not take credit the ability cannot substantiate.
Compounding is the primary mechanism. Little drops.
Incur no new debt.
Treat each person as a distinct individual.
Prepare before entering — but avoidance dressed as preparation is still avoidance.
Extract the lesson from every failure and record it.
Flexibility with the self has a limit. Know the difference between grace and the habit of letting the hook go.
Build toward freedom in order to give freely.
The present day is not a rehearsal.
Maintain beauty as an underlying condition of daily life.
Seek wisdom, not merely information.
Be dependable above being impressive.
Direction
Vision & Mission
The personal vision is where this life is pointed. The mission is how it gets there, day by day. The legacy statement is what remains after. These three together form the directional spine of the plan. Write them in your own words. Return to them when the days feel disconnected from the direction.
Personal Vision
The man this life is building toward
To become a man of complete integrity and deep faith — free in the fullest sense, structurally capable, and relationally generous — whose life constitutes a sustained argument that the individual, anchored in God and committed to their own becoming, is above their circumstances. And to build institutions that carry that argument past the limits of a single life.
Personal Mission
How the vision is lived daily
To show up fully every day — in faith, in craft, in character — compounding small acts of discipline and presence into a life of increasing freedom, depth, and impact. To be a man whose word can be trusted and whose work can be verified, and whose presence leaves people better than it found them.
Daily Mission
What today asks of you
Begin with God. Move the body. Feed the mind. Do the work. Leave something better than it was. Sleep having given the day its due. Repeat — compounding.
Legacy Statement
What remains
To leave a trail of lives awakened to their responsibility and ability to change — with institutions that inspire people to believe they deserve, and can do, better.

The argument, lived.

The institutions outlast the man. The Foundation continues. The companies continue. The children carry the values. The legacy is not a monument — it is a living argument, still being made by the people and structures that this life produced. A trail of lives awakened to their responsibility and ability to change.

The Shared Life
Together
This section is the part of the plan you build with Tamara — or at minimum, the part you bring to her. One of her complaints was that you didn't plan together. The answer to that is not to plan more privately and present it to her. It is to have a section of the plan that is genuinely hers to enter. This is that section. Write your part first. Then open the door.
What I Am Building Toward, With Her
The shared vision — your words first
Imagine five years of shared experiences. She moves to Port Harcourt. We're in a duplex on Sars Road — furnished properly, a kitchen that works, space for her to make things and space for me to build things. She is Creative Director in practice if not yet in documentation. We run ideas by each other before anyone else. She has Pyros. I have Dante Craft. We have one joint venture already underway — a building, a plot of land, something we own together. The household is one of balance and love and genuine partnership, both of us in step with God and with each other. No eggshells. No antennas. Just two people who chose each other on the hard days and compounded that choice into something neither of us could have built alone.
The Shared Timeline
Same city — target
Port Harcourt. She moves — her work is not location-bound. Not Abuja, not Lagos, not Ibadan. PH has the cost of living, the quality of life we can build, and my career is here for now. Target: she is here within a year of marriage. Duplex on Sars Road is the housing goal for year one. First visit end of Q2 2026 — flight funded, apartment ready for her. Quarterly visits (two months, two fortnights) until we're in the same city.
Marriage — target
December 2026 — propose. This requires monthly income of at least ₦1m, the debt fully resolved, the apartment complete, and the relationship in a rhythm that makes the question feel like the natural next step rather than a rescue. The conditions are financial stability and relational health, both together. She knows the direction. Tell her more often that you do too.
Children — intent
We agreed — no children. She doesn't want them. You, if you'd have them, never wanted more than two, and staying at none is settled. You wrote it yourself: "I've always thought to have my one person, untethered, unshared." That is the household. Two people fully committed to each other and to the work, without division. This is a decision made together, not a compromise — honour it as such.
Her place in the build
Creative Director — in practice even if not yet in documentation. R&D reports to her. She runs ideas by the work. Pyros (her food and making enterprise) sits alongside dAnTE Inc., not inside it — her own thing, fully hers. The dAnTE Foundation training program incorporates Pyros at the vocational level. You run every significant idea by her first. She forwarded you coal trailer specs with routes and pricing on a Saturday. She is already operating like a partner. Meet her there.
