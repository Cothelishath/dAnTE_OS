The work
Learning to love
the way a man should.
This is not a reading list. It is a curriculum — structured, sequenced, and built to produce change rather than just knowledge. Thirty minutes a day, five days a week. Read, reflect, bring it back. The other thirty minutes you apply it with a real person in real time.
Three tiers
Tier I — Foundation
Build yourself first.
You cannot lead what you haven't built. You cannot give what you don't have. These four pillars go deep into who you are before they touch how you relate to anyone else. Start here. Stay here until it's moving.
Leadership of Self Emotional Intelligence Vulnerability & Intimacy Spiritual Foundation
Tier II — Development
Build toward another person.
Once the foundation is in motion, these four pillars expand your capacity outward — toward how you show up, speak, fight, and understand the person you're building with.
Responsibility in Practice Communication Conflict & Repair Understanding Women
Tier III — Integration
These are practised, not studied.
The final three pillars cannot be learned from a book. They are the living out of everything that came before — in real moments, with a real person. The material here guides reflection. The practice is your actual life.
Connection Presence & Attention Service & Sacrifice
How this works
01
30 minutes, daily
This is your curriculum time inside the relationship research block. Short enough to protect. Consistent enough to compound. Don't negotiate with it.
02
One pillar at a time
You don't move to the next pillar until the current one has produced something in you — a shift in how you think, feel, or act. Not when you finish the book. When it's changed something.
03
Bring it back
After each resource, come back here and we discuss it. What landed. What didn't. What it exposed. What question it left you with. This is where the thinking gets tested.
04
Apply it the same day
The second 30 minutes of the research block is application — in real conversation, in real gestures, in how you show up. The curriculum lives in that gap between knowing and doing.
